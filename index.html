The Inspiration Behind Ikora

Ikora draws its wisdom from the Bhagavad Gita, a 5,000-year-old sacred Hindu scripture that is part of the epic Mahabharata. The Gita is a profound dialogue between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, where Arjuna faces a moral crisis about fighting in a war against his own relatives.

Through this conversation, Krishna imparts timeless wisdom about duty (dharma), righteousness, the nature of the self, and the paths to spiritual liberation. The teachings address universal human concerns that remain relevant today: dealing with anxiety, finding purpose, managing emotions, understanding loss, and achieving inner peace.

The name "Ikora" is inspired by the concept of inner wisdom and spiritual guidance. Just as Krishna guided Arjuna through his darkest moment, Ikora aims to guide you through life's challenges using these eternal principles adapted for the modern world.
